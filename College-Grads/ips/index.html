Investor Policy Statement

Things I Should Have Thought Of​

How can you determine how you're going to invest, if you haven't thought through the "Why" you're investing and your core philosphies.

I don't remember what random thread on the Internet I was on, when I saw this, and had the "D'oh" moment. So, this is the first pass at that.

Goals​

  • I need to provide for my family. This means:
    • My kids should have a very reasonable opportunity to graduate college without debt
      • I expect college to be a four year college at a reasonable out of state school or an in-state school and potentially graduate school.
      • I expect them to have a budget and to take on debt past that budget.
    • I require housing stability. Which is to say, paying for my housing should be extremely easy or the housing itself should be paid off.
  • I want to be able to retire, travel and have the flexibility to spend time where my kids end up.
    • Travel
      • I plan to "slow travel", which is to say, the ability to rent a place to stay for a month in a foreign location and enjoy the experience.
      • I expect to do this a few times a year, but hey, who can tell, maybe I'll hate it.
  • I expect that I need to have sufficient capital so that a 4% withdrawal rate can continue in-perpetuity.
  • I expect that I will die before my spouse does, simply because of prior history within the family.
  • I expect that I will require some level of nursing care for my final years.
    • This means I need savings to allow that to happen without being either a financial burden or an overwhelming emotional problem.
  • I will retire in my 50s.

Plan​

Given that I want to be able to withdrawal at 4%, I need 25X my planned annual withdrawal available in investments. These investments will be a mix of tax free, tax deferred and fully taxed accounts.

Philosophy​

I will attempt to maximize tax advantage without pursuing it at the cost of overall returns. i.e.

  • In years where I have high W-2 income I will attempt to put as much "pre-tax" money away as possible to reduce my tax burden.
  • Where possible I will utilize tax strategies that allow for the conversion of tax-deferred to tax-free without incurring a tax liability (aka Back Door Roth, Mega-Back Door Roth).
  • Saving money is taxable accounts is still saving money.

Investments​

Holy crap, this is the hard part.

Discussion​

There is an inherent conflict between the evidence on Safe Withdrawal Rates (SWR) and investing in the "Efficient Frontier". This is further complicated by your risk profile.

Details​

  • I will not invest in real estate that I'm not planning on living in. Real estate I'm planning on living in I will not rent out.
    • Why? Because it's too much work. I've invested in a handful of syndicated deals and you have low certainty, low liquidity and returns that generally mirror the market. Sure, people have been successful with it, but most people are better off in broad based stock funds.
    • So, if you want that, good for you. It's just not for me, which is great.
  • I will invest in index funds with low expense ratios, where low is defined as a goal of < 0.1%
  • I will have no more than 0.001% of my money in beer sitting in a beer fridge somewhere.
  • I will have no less than 10% of my money in bonds.
  • I will have no less than 10% of my money in international stocks.
  • I will rebalance my portfolio no less than once a year.

Why no less? Because it's easier for me to define minimums than it is to define the maximum of my domestic stock exposure. Should it be? No. But, did I ask your opinion? No. Write your own damn statement. :)
