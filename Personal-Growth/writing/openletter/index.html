An Open Letter to My Children

Well, you're getting older, I'm getting older, it's almost like that's how time works. At some point I'll be gone, and this will probably exist out on the internet before it decays with the rest of human civilization. I'm writing this down as my perspective. I'm going to be giving you the truth as I see it. Other people will have different truths and maybe theirs are better, but I think mine are pretty good.

Let's get something out of the way:

I. Love. You.

All of you. As you're growing up you think of love as a given. Sure, you love your parents, but they're always there. You know nothing else. Then you meet other people and fall in love with them, and then out of love. You love your pets. But children are different. I'm sure science can explain to us how evolution has manipulated our hormones so that seeing/being with you changes our emotions. None of that matters. Your love for your children is more absolute. You have more invested in them than anyone else. I think if you do this right, you're realize how pointlessness of the question of "Who is your favorite?" It's easy to see the world in terms of discrete answers. Yes. No. A. B. C. The world is messier than that with huge ranges of experiences and opinions. You're all different, and that's awesome.

We don't know what we're doing thought there were just adults and children

There isn't a manual, which annoys the crap out of me

Trying to maximize probabilities

Just want you to be successful that's not just money, but you do need money
